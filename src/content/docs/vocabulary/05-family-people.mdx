Dutch family vocabulary is essential for everyday conversation — the Dutch are famously direct about family life and relationships. This section covers close and extended family members, common relationship terms, professions, and adjectives for describing people. Note that some family terms have informal variants that are widely used in daily speech.

> **Tip**: *De neef* and *de nicht* each have two meanings: nephew/niece (your sibling's child) **and** male/female cousin (your aunt/uncle's child). Context usually makes the meaning clear.

## Mini Dialogue

> **Sophie**: Hoe groot is jouw familie, Mark?
> */hu ɣroːt ɪs jɑu faːˈmiːli mɑrk/*
> *(How big is your family, Mark?)*
>
> **Mark**: Best groot! Ik heb twee broers en een zus. En jij?
> */bɛst ɣroːt | ɪk hɛp tʋeː bruːrs ɛn ən zʏs | ɛn jɛi/*
> *(Quite big! I have two brothers and a sister. And you?)*
>
> **Sophie**: Ik heb alleen een oudere broer. Hij is getrouwd en heeft twee kinderen.
> */ɪk hɛp ɑˈleːn ən ˈɑudərə bruːr | hɛi ɪs ɣəˈtrɑut ɛn heːft tʋeː ˈkɪndərə(n)/*
> *(I only have an older brother. He is married and has two children.)*
>
> **Mark**: Oh, leuk! Dan ben je tante. Wat doet je broer?
> */oː løːk | dɑn bɛn jə ˈtɑntə | ʋɑt duːt jə bruːr/*
> *(Oh, nice! Then you're an aunt. What does your brother do?)*
>
> **Sophie**: Hij is ingenieur. Zijn vrouw is verpleegkundige. En jouw ouders?
> */hɛi ɪs ɪnʒəˈnøːr | zɛin vrɑu ɪs vərˈpleːxkʏndəɣə | ɛn jɑu ˈɑudərs/*
> *(He's an engineer. His wife is a nurse. And your parents?)*
>
> **Mark**: Mijn vader is gepensioneerd — hij was bakker. Mijn moeder is lerares.
> */mɛin ˈvaːdər ɪs ɣəpɛnsiˈuːnɛːrt | hɛi ʋɑs ˈbɑkər | mɛin ˈmuːdər ɪs ləraːˈrɛs/*
> *(My father is retired — he was a baker. My mother is a teacher.)*
>
> **Sophie**: Wat leuk! Mijn oma was ook lerares. Ze was heel aardig en slim.
> */ʋɑt løːk | mɛin ˈoːmaː ʋɑs oːk ləraːˈrɛs | zə ʋɑs heːl ˈaːrdəx ɛn slɪm/*
> *(How nice! My grandma was also a teacher. She was very kind and smart.)*
>
> **Mark**: Opa en oma zijn het beste! Mijn opa is al negentig, maar nog steeds heel grappig.
> */ˈoːpaː ɛn ˈoːmaː zɛin ɦɛt ˈbɛstə | mɛin ˈoːpaː ɪs ɑl ˈneːɣəntəx | maːr nɔx stiːts heːl ˈɣrɑpəx/*
> *(Grandpa and grandma are the best! My grandpa is already ninety, but still very funny.)*
