## Finding the Stem

The stem of a Dutch verb is the foundation of present-tense conjugation. To find the stem:

1. Take the **infinitive** (e.g., *werken*)
2. Remove **-en** → *werk*
3. Apply Dutch **spelling rules**:

| Rule | Infinitive | IPA | Raw Stem | Correct Stem | Why |
|------|-----------|-----|----------|-------------|-----|
| Double vowel in closed syllable | mak**en** | `/ˈmaː.kən/` | mak | **maak** | Long /aː/ needs two a's in a closed syllable |
| Double vowel in closed syllable | lev**en** | `/ˈleː.vən/` | lev | **leef** | Long /eː/ needs two e's, and v → f at word end |
| v → f at end of word | schrijv**en** | `/ˈsxrɛi̯.vən/` | schrijv | **schrijf** | v never ends a Dutch word |
| z → s at end of word | reiz**en** | `/ˈrɛi̯.zən/` | reiz | **reis** | z never ends a Dutch word |
| Single consonant | lop**en** | `/ˈloː.pən/` | lopp | **loop** | Double consonant simplified; long vowel restored |
| Single consonant | zitt**en** | `/ˈzɪ.tən/` | zitt | **zit** | Short vowel keeps single consonant in closed syllable |

## Conjugation Pattern

| Person | Ending | Example (*werken*) | Example (*maken*) |
|--------|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| ik | stem | ik werk | ik maak |
| jij/je | stem + **t** | jij werkt | jij maakt |
| u | stem + **t** | u werkt | u maakt |
| hij/zij/het | stem + **t** | hij werkt | hij maakt |
| wij/we | infinitive | wij werken | wij maken |
| jullie | infinitive | jullie werken | jullie maken |
| zij (they) | infinitive | zij werken | zij maken |

### Important: Inversion Rule for *jij/je*

When **jij/je** comes **after** the verb (in questions or inverted sentences), the **-t is dropped**:

| Normal | Inverted (question) |
|--------|-------------------|
| Jij werk**t** hier. | **Werk** jij hier? |
| Jij maak**t** dat. | **Maak** jij dat? |
| Jij lees**t** een boek. | **Lees** jij een boek? |

This rule applies **only** to jij/je — not to u, hij, or zij.

> **Note:** If the stem already ends in **-t**, the conjugated form stays the same for all singular persons: *zitten* → stem *zit* → jij zit (not jij zitt). In inversion nothing visibly changes: "Zit jij hier?"

## Separable Verbs

Many Dutch verbs are **separable** (*scheidbare werkwoorden*). They consist of a prefix + a base verb. In the present tense, the prefix separates and moves to the **end** of the clause.

### Common Separable Verbs

| Infinitive | IPA | English | Prefix | Base Verb |
|-----------|-----|---------|--------|-----------|
| opstaan | `/ˈɔp.staːn/` | to get up | op | staan |
| meenemen | `/ˈmeː.neː.mən/` | to take along | mee | nemen |
| aankomen | `/ˈaːn.koː.mən/` | to arrive | aan | komen |
| weggaan | `/ˈʋɛx.ɣaːn/` | to leave/go away | weg | gaan |
| terugkomen | `/təˈrʏx.koː.mən/` | to come back | terug | komen |
| opbellen | `/ˈɔp.bɛ.lən/` | to phone/call | op | bellen |
| binnenkomen | `/ˈbɪ.nən.koː.mən/` | to come in | binnen | komen |
| uitnodigen | `/ˈœy̯t.noː.dəɣ.ən/` | to invite | uit | nodigen |
| meebrengen | `/ˈmeː.brɛŋ.ən/` | to bring along | mee | brengen |
| afspreken | `/ˈɑf.spreː.kən/` | to arrange/agree | af | spreken |

### How They Work in Sentences

The **base verb** stays in the normal verb position (second position), and the **prefix** goes to the end:

| Infinitive | Sentence | IPA | English |
|-----------|----------|-----|---------|
| opstaan | Ik **sta** elke dag om 7 uur **op**. | `/ɪk staː ˈɛl.kə dɑx ɔm ˈzeː.vən yːr ɔp/` | I get up every day at 7 o'clock. |
| meenemen | Hij **neemt** zijn tas **mee**. | `/hɛi̯ neːmt zɛi̯n tɑs meː/` | He takes his bag along. |
| aankomen | De trein **komt** om 9 uur **aan**. | `/də trɛi̯n kɔmt ɔm ˈneː.ɣən yːr aːn/` | The train arrives at 9 o'clock. |
| weggaan | Wij **gaan** morgen **weg**. | `/ʋɛi̯ ɣaːn ˈmɔr.ɣən ʋɛx/` | We are leaving tomorrow. |
| terugkomen | Zij **komt** volgende week **terug**. | `/zɛi̯ kɔmt ˈvɔl.ɣən.də ʋeːk təˈrʏx/` | She comes back next week. |
| opbellen | Ik **bel** je vanavond **op**. | `/ɪk bɛl jə ˈvaːn.aː.vɔnt ɔp/` | I'll call you this evening. |

### In Questions

The same separation applies — the prefix still goes to the end:

- **Sta** jij altijd zo vroeg **op**? — Do you always get up so early?
- **Neem** jij je boek **mee**? — Are you taking your book along?
- **Komt** de trein op tijd **aan**? — Does the train arrive on time?

> **Tip:** When the separable verb is used with a modal verb (*kunnen*, *moeten*, *willen*, etc.), it stays together as an infinitive at the end: *Ik moet om 6 uur **opstaan**.* — I have to get up at 6 o'clock.
