Dutch has surprisingly consistent patterns once you learn the rules. This page collects the most useful generalizations — apply them broadly and you'll be right most of the time.

## Spelling Rules

### Double vs. Single Vowels

The core Dutch spelling rule: **a single vowel in a closed syllable is short; a double vowel or a vowel in an open syllable is long.**

| Pattern | Example | IPA | Explanation |
|---------|---------|-----|-------------|
| CVC (closed, short) | man | `/mɑn/` | Short *a* — closed syllable |
| CVVC (double, long) | maan | `/maːn/` | Long *aa* — double vowel makes it long |
| CV-CV (open, long) | ma-nen | `/ˈmaː.nən/` | Long *a* — open syllable, no doubling needed |

This means when you add suffixes, spelling changes:

| Singular | Plural | Why |
|----------|--------|-----|
| maan | manen | Open syllable keeps the long *a*, drop one *a* |
| man | mannen | Double the *n* to keep the syllable closed (short *a*) |
| boot | boten | Open syllable keeps the long *o*, drop one *o* |
| bot | botten | Double the *t* to keep the syllable closed (short *o*) |

### The DT Rule

The most notorious Dutch spelling rule. For verbs with stems ending in *-d*:

| Person | Verb (*worden*) | Why |
|--------|----------------|-----|
| ik | word | Stem only |
| jij | wordt | Stem + t |
| hij/zij | wordt | Stem + t |
| wij | worden | Infinitive |

**The rule:** *Jij/hij* always gets **-t** even if the stem already ends in *-d*. So *wordt*, not *word*. In inversion (*word jij*), the *-t* drops because *jij* follows the verb.

## Word Formation

### Common Prefixes

Dutch loves prefixes. These work across hundreds of verbs and adjectives:

| Dutch | English | Example | Example Sentence |
|-------|---------|---------|-----------------|
| on- | un- / not | onmogelijk (impossible) | Dat is onmogelijk! *(That's impossible!)* |
| ver- | change / away | verhuizen (to move house) | Wij verhuizen naar Amsterdam. *(We're moving to Amsterdam.)* |
| be- | makes transitive | bespreken (to discuss) | We bespreken het plan. *(We discuss the plan.)* |
| ge- | past participle | gemaakt (made) | Ik heb het gemaakt. *(I made it.)* |
| ont- | un-/de- | ontdekken (to discover) | Hij heeft Amerika ontdekt. *(He discovered America.)* |
| her- | re- | herhalen (to repeat) | Kun je dat herhalen? *(Can you repeat that?)* |
| voor- | pre-/for- | voorkomen (to prevent) | We moeten dat voorkomen. *(We must prevent that.)* |
| aan- | on/to | aankomen (to arrive) | De trein komt aan. *(The train arrives.)* |
| uit- | out/off | uitleggen (to explain) | Kun je het uitleggen? *(Can you explain it?)* |
| op- | up/open | opbellen (to call) | Ik bel je op. *(I'll call you.)* |
| af- | off/down | afspreken (to arrange) | We spreken om drie uur af. *(We arrange to meet at three.)* |
| mee- | along/with | meenemen (to take along) | Neem je paraplu mee! *(Take your umbrella along!)* |
| terug- | back | terugkomen (to come back) | Ik kom morgen terug. *(I'm coming back tomorrow.)* |

### Common Suffixes

| Dutch | Function | Example | English |
|-------|----------|---------|---------|
| -heid | noun (state) | vrijheid | freedom |
| -lijk | adjective | vriendelijk | friendly |
| -ig | adjective | rustig | calm |
| -baar | adjective (-able) | betaalbaar | affordable |
| -loos | adjective (-less) | dakloos | homeless |
| -schap | noun (state) | vriendschap | friendship |
| -ster | female agent | schrijfster | female writer |
| -er | male agent / comparative | bakker, groter | baker, bigger |
| -je / -tje | diminutive | huisje | little house |
| -en | plural / infinitive | huizen, werken | houses, to work |
| -ing | noun (action) | vergadering | meeting |
| -tie / -sie | noun (from Latin) | informatie | information |

### Compound Words

Dutch builds long words by combining shorter ones. **The last word determines the gender and meaning:**

| Dutch | Components | English |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| ziekenhuis | ziek + en + huis | hospital (sick + house) |
| huisarts | huis + arts | family doctor (house + doctor) |
| handschoen | hand + schoen | glove (hand + shoe) |
| koelkast | koel + kast | fridge (cool + cupboard) |
| stofzuiger | stof + zuiger | vacuum cleaner (dust + sucker) |
| brandblusser | brand + blusser | fire extinguisher (fire + extinguisher) |
| verjaardagstaart | verjaardag + s + taart | birthday cake |
| zonnebloem | zon + ne + bloem | sunflower (sun + flower) |

**Linking letters** (*-s-*, *-e-*, *-en-*) often appear between components: verjaardags**s**taart, zonn**e**bloem, ziek**en**huis.

## Gender Patterns (de / het)

No perfect rule exists, but these patterns are right **most of the time**:

### Almost always *het*

| Pattern | Example | English |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Diminutives (-je) | het huisje | the little house |
| Languages | het Nederlands | Dutch |
| Sports & games | het voetbal | football |
| Metals | het goud | gold |
| Compass directions | het noorden | the north |
| Words starting with *ge-*, *be-*, *ver-* (many) | het gebouw | the building |
| Two-syllable *-el*, *-em*, *-en* (many) | het meisje, het water | the girl, the water |

### Almost always *de*

| Pattern | Example | English |
|---------|---------|---------|
| People (male/female) | de man, de vrouw | the man, the woman |
| Plural nouns (all!) | de huizen | the houses |
| -heid | de vrijheid | freedom |
| -ing | de vergadering | the meeting |
| -ie / -tie | de politie | the police |
| -schap | de vriendschap | friendship |
| -teit | de universiteit | the university |
| -ij | de bakkerij | the bakery |
| -eur | de computer | the computer |
| Trees, fruits, plants | de appel, de eik | the apple, the oak |
| Rivers, mountains | de Rijn, de Alpen | the Rhine, the Alps |

## Plural Rules

Dutch has two main plural endings: **-en** and **-s**.

### Use *-en* (most words)

| Singular | Plural | Rule |
|----------|--------|------|
| huis | huizen | Default: add -en (z before vowel) |
| boot | boten | Long vowel: drop double letter in open syllable |
| man | mannen | Short vowel: double consonant to keep it short |
| dag | dagen | Default: add -en |

### Use *-s* (words ending in unstressed syllable)

| Singular | Plural | Rule |
|----------|--------|------|
| tafel | tafels | Ends in -el |
| jongen | jongens | Ends in -en |
| meisje | meisjes | Diminutives always -s |
| auto | auto's | Foreign words / vowel ending: use apostrophe-s |
| café | cafés | Foreign loanwords |

### Irregular

| Singular | Plural | Dutch |
|----------|--------|-------|
| kind | kinderen | child → children |
| ei | eieren | egg → eggs |
| stad | steden | city → cities |

## Verb Patterns

### Past Participle (ge- + stem + -d/-t)

The `'t kofschip` rule determines the ending: if the **stem** ends in one of *t, k, f, s, ch, p*, the past participle ends in **-t**. Otherwise **-d**.

| Infinitive | Stem | 't kofschip? | Past Participle | English |
|-----------|------|-------------|-----------------|---------|
| werken | werk | **k** → yes | gewerkt | worked |
| fietsen | fiets | **s** → yes | gefietst | cycled |
| maken | maak | **k** → yes | gemaakt | made |
| leven | leef | **f** → yes | geleefd | lived |
| wonen | woon | no | gewoond | lived |
| spelen | speel | no | gespeeld | played |

**Mnemonic:** *'t **k**o**f****s****ch**i**p*** — if the stem ends in any letter from this word, use -t.

### Separable Verbs

Verbs with stressed prefixes split in main clauses:

| Infinitive | Sentence | English |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| opbellen | Ik bel je op. | I call you. |
| meenemen | Neem je jas mee! | Take your coat along! |
| terugkomen | Hij komt morgen terug. | He comes back tomorrow. |

**In subordinate clauses**, they stay together: *...dat ik je **opbel**.* *(that I call you)*

### Common Strong Verb Patterns

| Pattern | Infinitive | Past | Past Participle | English |
|---------|-----------|------|-----------------|---------|
| ij → ee → e | schrijven | schreef | geschreven | to write |
| ie → oo → o | schieten | schoot | geschoten | to shoot |
| i → a → o | drinken | dronk | gedronken | to drink |
| e → a → o | spreken | sprak | gesproken | to speak |

## Pronunciation Patterns

### Final Devoicing

Voiced consonants become voiceless at the end of a word:

| Written | Pronounced | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| -d | /t/ | hond → /hɔnt/ |
| -b | /p/ | heb → /hɛp/ |
| -g | /x/ | dag → /dɑx/ |
| -v | /f/ | leef (from leven) |
| -z | /s/ | huis → /hœys/ |

### Cognate Spelling Shifts (English → Dutch)

If you know English, these patterns help you guess Dutch spellings:

| English | Dutch | Example |
|---------|-------|---------|
| -tion | -tie | information → informatie |
| -ly | -lijk | friendly → vriendelijk |
| -ty | -teit | university → universiteit |
| -ous | -eus | famous → fameus |
| c- | k- | cat → kat |
| th- | d- | three → drie |
| sh- | sch- | ship → schip |
| oo | oe | school → school, cool → koel |

## Sentence Structure Patterns

### Main Clause: Verb Second (V2)

The conjugated verb is **always in position 2** in main clauses:

| Position 1 | Verb | Rest |
|-----------|------|------|
| Ik | werk | vandaag. |
| Vandaag | werk | ik thuis. |
| Morgen | gaan | wij naar Amsterdam. |

### Subordinate Clause: Verb Last

After conjunctions like *dat, omdat, als, wanneer, toen*, the verb goes to the end:

| Example | English |
|---------|---------|
| Ik weet dat hij morgen komt. | I know that he comes tomorrow. |
| Omdat het regent, blijf ik thuis. | Because it's raining, I stay home. |

### Time-Manner-Place

Dutch follows **time → manner → place** order (like German):

| Example | English |
|---------|---------|
| Ik ga morgen met de trein naar Amsterdam. | I go tomorrow by train to Amsterdam. |

---

> **Tip:** These patterns won't cover every exception, but they'll help you make educated guesses. When in doubt, the patterns on this page will be right roughly 80-90% of the time. The exceptions are worth learning individually as you encounter them.
